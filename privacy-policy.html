Privacy Policy

Effective Date: December 20, 2025

Last Updated: December 20, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how Accel Analysis, LLC (d/b/a AccelMail) (“AccelMail,” “we,” “us,” “our”) collects, uses, discloses, and protects information when you access or use our Services.

1) Scope

This policy applies to:

  • Our websites, apps, and platform features that link to this policy
  • Campaign tools, QR codes we generate/host, and related analytics dashboards

This policy does not cover third-party websites/services you may access through links or integrations.

2) Roles: Business Users and Recipient Data

AccelMail is typically a service provider/processor for Recipient Data that our business customers upload (names, addresses, segmentation tags, etc.). Our customers control what they upload and how they use it. If you are a recipient of a mailer and want to exercise rights about a business’s list, you may need to contact the business that mailed you.

3) Information We Collect

A) Information you provide to us

  • Account info: name, email, phone, password (hashed), role, company name
  • Billing info: billing contact details and transaction records (payment card data is typically handled by our payment processor)
  • Support and communications: messages, attachments, recorded call notes (if applicable)
  • Content you upload: recipient lists, creative files, offers, URLs, campaign configurations

B) Information collected automatically

  • Device and usage data: IP address, browser type, device identifiers, pages viewed, actions taken, timestamps, referring URLs
  • Cookies and similar technologies: for authentication, preferences, security, and analytics
  • Log data: error logs, audit trails, and security events

C) QR code and campaign tracking data

When a QR code is scanned (or its URL is visited), we may collect:

  • Timestamp and scan/visit events
  • IP address and approximate location (derived from IP, not precise GPS)
  • Device/browser information and referrer data
  • The campaign/QR identifier and destination URL

D) Information from integrations (if enabled)

If you connect third-party services (e.g., spreadsheets/CRM/calendar), we may receive metadata and content necessary to perform the integration, based on your settings and permissions.

4) How We Use Information

We use information to:

  • Provide, operate, maintain, and secure the Services
  • Process payments and manage subscriptions
  • Fulfill Orders and campaign workflows (including print/mail coordination if offered)
  • Generate QR codes and analytics, reporting, and attribution insights
  • Respond to support requests and communicate service updates
  • Monitor for fraud, abuse, and security incidents
  • Improve and develop new features (including aggregated performance insights)

5) Legal Bases (For EEA/UK Where Applicable)

Where required, we rely on:

  • Contract necessity (to provide the Services)
  • Legitimate interests (security, fraud prevention, improving Services)
  • Consent (where required for certain cookies/marketing)
  • Legal obligations (tax, compliance, lawful requests)

6) How We Share Information

We may share information with:

  • Service providers (hosting, analytics, customer support tools, payment processors, email delivery, mapping/geo services) under contractual confidentiality and security obligations.
  • Printing/mailing partners (if applicable) to produce and mail campaign materials.
  • Legal/compliance: to comply with law, subpoenas, or valid government requests.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets.
  • With your direction: when you enable integrations or instruct us to share.

We do not sell personal information

We do not sell personal information in exchange for money. If laws define “sale” or “share” broadly (e.g., certain targeted advertising contexts), we structure our disclosures as service-provider processing, and we offer choices where applicable.

7) Cookies and Tracking Choices

We use cookies for:

  • Authentication and session management
  • Preferences and feature enablement
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Analytics and performance

You can control cookies through browser settings. Some features may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

(If you run ads or cross-site tracking, add a dedicated “Cookie Notice” + opt-in/out banner logic.)

8) Data Retention

We retain information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide the Services and your Account
  • Maintain records for legitimate business purposes (audit trails, dispute resolution)
  • Comply with legal obligations

You may request deletion subject to contractual, operational, and legal limits (see Section 11).

9) Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect information (e.g., access controls, encryption in transit, least-privilege). No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; therefore, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10) Children’s Privacy

The Services are not directed to children under 13 (and not intended for minors). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child provided information, contact us to remove it.

11) Your Privacy Rights (Depending on Location)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights such as:

  • Access/confirm we process your personal information
  • Correct inaccuracies
  • Delete certain personal information
  • Obtain a copy (data portability)
  • Opt out of certain processing (e.g., targeted advertising, profiling, or certain sharing where applicable)
  • Appeal a refusal to take action (where required)

How to submit a request

Email: [privacy@yourdomain.com] with the subject “Privacy Request” and include:

  • Your name and email
  • The request type (access/correct/delete/port/opt-out)
  • Enough detail for us to verify and locate relevant data

We may need to verify identity before fulfilling requests. If you are acting as an authorized agent, we may request proof of authorization.

12) Marketing Communications

We may send Service-related messages (billing, security, operational notices).

You may opt out of promotional emails by using the unsubscribe link, but you may still receive non-promotional operational messages.

13) International Transfers

If you access the Services from outside the U.S., your information may be processed in the United States or other jurisdictions where our providers operate. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards.

14) Third-Party Links and Services

Our Services may link to third-party sites or enable integrations. Their privacy practices are governed by their policies, not ours.

15) Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will post the updated version with a new “Last Updated” date. Material changes may be communicated through the Services or email.
